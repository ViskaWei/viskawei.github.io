Robust Radial-Velocity Estimation from Low‑S/N Stellar Spectra
Large spectroscopic surveys and time‑domain follow‑up are limited by unreliable radial‑velocity (RV) estimates at low signal‑to‑noise (S/N). We developed a 1‑D U‑Net blind‑spot denoiser that ingests flux and per‑pixel uncertainties and outputs a denoised spectrum in real time. The method requires no clean training targets, preserves absorption‑line profiles, and is designed for robust operation where conventional template‑fitting pipelines fail.
Key quantitative results for RV estimation from denoised spectra:
* Precision: the standard deviation of RV residuals, σ(ΔRV), improves by a factor of 2–4 across S/N and closely follows the photon‑noise limit .
